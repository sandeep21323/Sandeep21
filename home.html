The Gospel of Jesus Christ

What is the Gospel of Jesus Christ?

The Gospel is the "Good News" that Jesus Christ, the Son of God, came into the world to save sinners. He lived a perfect life, died on the cross for our sins, and rose again from the dead to give us eternal life.

"For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life." – John 3:16
"Moreover, brethren, I declare unto you the gospel... that Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures; and that he was buried, and that he rose again the third day..." – 1 Corinthians 15:1-4

What is the Church?

The Church is not a building, but a community of believers in Jesus Christ who are called out to worship, serve, and proclaim Him. The Church is the Body of Christ, with Christ as its head.

"Now ye are the body of Christ, and members in particular." – 1 Corinthians 12:27
"And I say also unto thee... upon this rock I will build my church; and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it." – Matthew 16:18

Purpose of the Church (According to the Holy Bible)

  • Worship God: To glorify and honor God in spirit and in truth (John 4:24).
  • Preach the Gospel: To proclaim salvation through Jesus Christ to all nations (Mark 16:15).
  • Teach the Word: To disciple believers and teach the Scriptures (Matthew 28:19-20).
  • Fellowship: To encourage one another in faith and love (Hebrews 10:24-25).
  • Service: To care for the needy and support one another (Galatians 6:10).
